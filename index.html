Bolivia: Regulatory & Partnership Overview

Strategic Briefing for Shareholders & Government Meeting Preparation (2026)

Executive Summary: The Bolivian gambling sector is currently in a high-growth "formalization" phase[cite: 12]. With over 1,200 active licenses and a comprehensive 2026 reform agenda under President Rodrigo Paz, the state is actively seeking strategic partnerships to modernize the national lottery and scratchcard verticals[cite: 12, 28].

1. Institutional Landscape & Legal Framework

Bolivia’s framework is strictly state-controlled, governed primarily by Law No. 060 (2010)[cite: 3].

  • Autoridad de Fiscalización del Juego (AJ): The central regulator responsible for licensing, supervision, and enforcement[cite: 4, 8]. It operates under the Ministry of Economy and Public Finance[cite: 9].
  • Lotería Nacional (LONABOL): The state institution historically responsible for national beneficence lotteries and health programs[cite: 6, 10].
  • Enforcement: AJ uses a significant budget for inspections, specifically targeting illegal online operations[cite: 13].

2. Commercial Status: Lottery & Scratchcards

Lottery operations fall under state domain, but private participation is a historical and current reality[cite: 17].

  • Modern Structure: Formally integrated under Law No. 060, including paper-based and electronic lottery solutions[cite: 15, 16].
  • Partnership Models: Participation typically occurs via concessions or contracts under the LONABOL umbrella[cite: 6, 18].
  • Sensitivity: Due to past contractual difficulties with private entities, transparency and robust governance are now paramount for new partnerships[cite: 18].

4. The 2026 Digital Reform

President Rodrigo Paz's administration is advancing a modernization roadmap.

  • Digitalization: Plans to digitalize the regulator and create a framework for online betting.
  • Grey Area Transition: Efforts are underway to move from a "legal grey area" in online gambling to a formalized licensing track[cite: 25, 26, 58].
  • Opportunity: This reform creates a window for compliant digital lottery and scratchcard pilot programs[cite: 28, 56].
