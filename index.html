1. Overview of Bolivian Gambling Framework

Bolivia's gambling sector operates under a strict, state-controlled framework designed to channel gaming activities into a formalized economy while actively penalizing unauthorized operations .

Primary Legislation: The sector is governed by Law No. 060 of 2010, which covers lotteries, games of chance, raffles, and other gambling operations . Under this law, any gaming activity that is not explicitly authorized is strictly prohibited .

Key Institutions & Their Roles

  • Autoridad de Fiscalización del Juego (AJ): Established by Law No. 060 and attached to the Ministry of Economy and Public Finance, the AJ is the central regulator and licensing entity for all lotteries, games of chance, promotional campaigns, and gaming machine operations in Bolivia . The AJ is responsible for supervising compliance, carrying out audits, and issuing sanctions .
  • Lotería Nacional de Beneficencia y Salubridad (LONABOL / LNB): The historic state body responsible for administering the national lottery and charitable lotteries, with proceeds earmarked for health and social welfare programs . Private participation historically takes the form of concessions or management contracts operating under its legal umbrella .
  • Ministry of Economy and Public Finance (MEFP): The political and supervising authority above the AJ, responsible for designing fiscal policies, gaming tax structures, and aligning the gaming sector with the country's economic strategy .

Market Formalization & Scale

Recent regulatory reporting indicates that Bolivia currently hosts more than 1,200 active licenses across various promotional and gaming activities . This is part of a deliberate government push to utilize regulated gaming as a driver for economic recovery, formalization, and increased fiscal collection . Consequently, the AJ has directed a substantial portion of its resources toward enforcement, inspections, and cracking down on illegal physical and online gambling platforms .

2. Status of the Lottery and Scratchcard Business

Traditional lotteries in Bolivia have deep historical roots dating back to the nineteenth century . The modern institutional structure was formalized in 2002 and later consolidated under the broader regulatory mandates of Law No. 060 in 2010 .

Current Status & Distribution Channels

  • State Gatekeeping: Under Law No. 060, all lottery and scratchcard products are under state domain, meaning the Lotería Nacional and AJ serve as the ultimate gatekeepers for any licensing, concession, or partnership discussions with private companies .
  • Operational Background: Historically, the state lottery has relied on traditional paper-based formats with lower regional turnover rates . However, the state has experimented with electronic lottery systems, televised draws, and ticket vending machines .
  • Governance Risks: Historically, LONABOL has outsourced or granted operational rights to private entities, some of which suffered from financial and contractual compliance issues . For new partnerships, demonstrating a robust governance and transparent compliance framework is absolutely critical to the authorities .

3. Taxation and Economic Considerations

Bolivia implements a structured, vertical-specific taxation system to extract fiscal value from the gaming industry while coordinating collection directly through the national tax administration .

Gaming Vertical Tax Treatment / Rate Strategic Context
Lotteries & Scratchcards ~15% of GGR Taxed at a moderate rate to encourage participation and support social welfare programs .
Casinos & Gaming Halls ~30% of GGR Subject to higher fiscal pressure to balance social impacts .
Promotional Games Lower Tier / Variable Lower tax burden to stimulate temporary retail and promotional activations .

Macro Market Outlook

The total annual market size of the Bolivian gaming sector is estimated in the range of several hundred million US dollars . The current administration views regulated gaming as an important economic driver . This creates a strategic entry window where potential partners can argue for modernized operations, highlighting benefits such as direct technology transfer, local employment, and enhanced revenue collection for social welfare programs through updated lottery systems .

4. Online and Digital Channels

Online and digital distribution channels are currently positioned in a transitional regulatory phase, presenting both compliance risks and lucrative opportunities for compliant operators .

The Existing Legal Grey Area

Law No. 060 explicitly bans any form of unauthorized online gambling, but it lacks a clearly defined licensing process or operating framework for online sportsbooks, casinos, or digital lotteries . While foreign online platforms still actively accept Bolivian players, the AJ and government are pushing to close this gap to capture missing tax revenue and protect players .

The 2026 Reform Context

Under the administration of President Rodrigo Paz, the government is moving forward with a major digital modernization reform package in 2026 . This initiative is expected to include:

  • Full digitalization of the AJ regulatory tracking systems .
  • Adjustments or elimination of certain gaming taxes to encourage legal operators .
  • A formal licensing framework for online and digital betting .
This reform process opens a massive strategic window to introduce compliant digital lottery and scratchcard products .

5. Operational and Partnership Considerations

A partnership or takeover of lottery and scratchcard operations in Bolivia requires addressing key legal and technical parameters beyond standard compliance structures :

  • Concession vs. Licensing Models: Clarifying whether the state lottery intends to establish a long-term concession model, a Joint Venture with LONABOL, or standard AJ-issued product licenses .
  • Exclusivity and Terms: Establishing whether the operator will enjoy nationwide exclusivity for physical and digital scratchcard and lottery games, and under what exact renewal performance targets .
  • Retail Footprint: Defining minimum coverage expectations, geographic network mandates (including rural and remote access targets), and POS limitations (e.g., kiosks, gas stations, pharmacies) .
  • Technology Certifications: Mapping out regulatory technical standards for central systems, Random Number Generators (RNGs), instant ticket validation systems, security printing, and local server hosting or international lab validation approvals .
  • Earmarking and Social Earmarks: Structuring compliance workflows to guarantee that the correct statutory portion of revenues flows seamlessly to national health and social welfare programs .
  • Reporting and Auditing: Setting up direct, automated reporting channels to AJ and LONABOL to manage audit scopes and mitigate enforcement risk .

Q A.3 Is market exclusivity envisioned for the strategic partner role, and if so, for what term and under what performance or renewal criteria?

Q D.2 Are there legal restrictions on retail point-of-sale formats, and how are commercial retailer commissions or agent agreements structured and regulated?
