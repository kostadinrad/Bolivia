II. Executive Summary

Bolivia’s gaming sector operates under Law No. 060 (2010), which establishes a state-controlled model where all non-authorized gambling is illegal. The Autoridad de Fiscalización del Juego (AJ), under the Ministry of Economy and Public Finance, oversees licensing and supervision for lotteries, raffles, gaming machines and related products. A 2026 reform agenda, driven by the current administration, aims to modernize AJ, expand digital controls and clarify a future online licensing framework, opening a window for structured private partnerships in the lottery and scratchcard space.

The national lottery body (often referred to as Lotería Nacional / LONABOL) has historically operated charitable lotteries and experimented with electronic lottery formats, with proceeds earmarked for social and health programs. The regulated market, covering casinos, lotteries and other games, is estimated in the USD 300–500M range annually, with over 1,200 active licenses issued by AJ. The government explicitly views regulated gaming as an economic driver and a tool for fiscal collection and formalization.

III. Core Regulatory and Market Picture

The Ministry of Economy and Public Finance is the political authority supervising AJ and shaping gambling tax policy. Under Law 060, any gambling not expressly authorized is prohibited, with sanctions ranging from fines to license revocation and criminal charges for illegal operations.

  • Autoridad de Fiscalización del Juego (AJ): Central regulator for all games of chance, lotteries, raffles, gaming machines and promotional games. AJ manages licensing, inspections and sanctions and currently supervises more than 1,200 active licenses across the country.
  • Lotería Nacional / LONABOL: Historic state lottery entity in charge of the National Lottery and charitable draws. It has precedent for outsourcing operations to private partners via concessions or management contracts, particularly for electronic lottery initiatives.
  • Market Scale & Direction: The regulated gambling market is estimated at several hundred million USD per year, and the government is actively using licensing expansion and enforcement against illegal operations to drive formalization and tax revenue.

IV. Lottery & Scratchcard Business Landscape

Bolivia’s national lottery has historical roots going back more than a century, with the modern National Lottery framework formalized in the early 2000s and subsequently integrated under Law 060. Traditional paper-based draws coexist with electronic lottery initiatives launched in past years.

  • State Control: Lottery and instant-win products are treated as a state domain. Private participation typically occurs through concessions, management contracts or licenses tied to the state lottery entity and supervised by AJ.
  • Past Outsourcing: There is historical precedent for partnering with private operators, though some past agreements suffered from financial and performance issues, making governance, transparency and compliance critical for any new structure.
  • Product Evolution: The market has seen experiments with electronic lottery terminals, televised draws and accumulated jackpots, but scratchcards and digital distribution remain underdeveloped relative to regional benchmarks.

V. Online & Digital Channels

Law 060 prohibits unauthorized online gambling, and Bolivia does not yet have a fully defined licensing regime for online casino or sportsbook operators. Nonetheless, foreign online operators continue to accept Bolivian players, and AJ has publicly acknowledged the need to update the framework.

  • Legal Grey Zone: Online gambling is not explicitly licensed; regulators treat unauthorized online offers as illegal, but enforcement is challenging.
  • Reform Momentum: Since around 2020, AJ and government stakeholders have signaled intention to amend Law 060 to introduce clear online licensing rules, improve tax collection and enhance consumer protection.
  • 2026 Agenda: The current administration’s 2026 agenda includes digitalization of AJ’s systems and a new structure for online betting and gaming, creating an opportunity to propose regulated digital lottery ticket sales and instant-win products as controlled pilots.

VI. Compliance Function & Governance

A dedicated Regulatory Research & Compliance Officer is central to successful entry and long-term operation in Bolivia. The role connects multi-jurisdictional expertise with local requirements.

  • Regulatory Mapping: Analyze Law 060, AJ regulations, lottery-specific norms and tax rules and align them with company products and processes (lotto, scratchcards, promotional games, digital channels).
  • Policy Framework: Develop and implement AML/KYC, responsible gaming and marketing compliance policies tailored to Bolivian law and cultural context, ensuring consistency across retail and digital distribution.
  • Monitoring & Reporting: Track AJ resolutions, enforcement trends and tax changes; coordinate internal audits, risk assessments and regulatory filings; maintain structured communication with AJ and the state lottery entity.
  • Training & Culture: Provide ongoing training for operational and commercial teams on local compliance obligations and reporting standards.

VII. Key Partnership Design Questions

This bank groups concrete questions to structure the government meeting for a potential lotto & scratchcard partnership.

A. Regulatory & Licensing Model

Under Law 060, what legal vehicle does the government prefer for private participation in the national lottery and scratchcard portfolio (concession, PPP, joint venture with the state lottery, or standard AJ license)?
What would be the precise scope of authorization (national lotto, instant scratchcards, raffles, sports-linked lotto, promotional games)?
Is exclusivity envisaged for specific products or for nationwide lottery operations, and for what duration and renewal conditions?
What fit-and-proper and technical suitability criteria will apply to shareholders, directors and key staff?

B. Financial & Tax Structure

Which taxes and contributions will apply to lotto and scratchcards (GGR tax, VAT, municipal fees, earmarked social contributions)?
Are any adjustments to lottery tax rates or structures being studied as part of the 2026 reform?
How are proceeds currently allocated between prizes, state beneficiaries and operator costs, and how flexible is this structure?
Does the government prefer a revenue-share, minimum-guarantee plus variable, or other hybrid model for a private partner?

C. Online & Digital Transition

What is the envisioned timeline and structure for a new online licensing framework, particularly for digital lottery ticket sales and instant-win products?
Is there openness to sandbox or pilot schemes where a trusted operator can test digital channels under AJ supervision?
Will digital lottery and scratchcard products be taxed identically to retail lottery or under a differentiated regime?

D. Operations & Distribution

What minimum network size and geographic coverage does the government expect for points of sale (including rural and remote areas)?
Are there any restrictions on the types of merchants who may host lottery terminals and sell scratchcards?
What service-level and incident-response standards will AJ require from lottery systems and terminal infrastructure?

E. Compliance, AML & Responsible Gaming

What specific AML/KYC requirements apply to lottery operations (ID thresholds for prize payouts, suspicious transaction reporting, record-keeping periods)?
How does AJ currently enforce responsible gaming measures in the lottery segment (age verification, self-exclusion, limits, advertising controls)?
What data access and reporting cadence (real-time feeds, daily files, monthly reports) are expected for operational, financial and RG metrics?

F. Governance & Collaboration

How will governance be structured between AJ, the state lottery entity and a private partner (steering committees, technical working groups, escalation processes)?
What types of preventive compliance measures and transparency practices does the regulator value most to minimize enforcement actions?

G. Reform Agenda & Long-Term Vision

How does the 2026 digitalization and reform of Law 060 connect to the long-term vision for the national lottery and instant-win portfolio?
What key performance indicators (fiscal, social, technological) will the government use to evaluate the success of a partnership over 5–10 years?

H. Social Policy, CSR & Local Footprint

Which social and health programs are currently prioritized for funding from lottery revenues, and how can a private partner best contribute to their expansion?
What expectations exist regarding local employment, training, technology transfer and engagement of local suppliers?
Is the government interested in joint public campaigns against illegal gambling and for responsible play, particularly in the context of expanding digital access?
